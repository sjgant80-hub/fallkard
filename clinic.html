The Clinic.
An organism reads the body it built — and heals.

A bred agent that only ever wins is a provably-fit cripple: it holds three shapes and dies at the generation wall, because the two it lacks are the two that persist a self. The Clinic adds them. It reads every match as seven tells — grounded in the reducer's own moves, never borrowed labels — and keeps a lifetime journal. The loudest tell is the organism's shadow. Fork toward the shadow, select against it, and the population heals — provably faster than blind evolution. The ones that hold all five shapes cast back as real cards. The game grows its own gene pool.

5 · The mint-back — organisms cast as cards

matured champions, content-addressed by composition · the loop closes on itself
A card's identity is the hash of its composition, not the organism's wallet — so casting the same discovered stack twice returns the byte-identical card (—). atk is earned from resilience (1 − shadow), hp from bloodline depth, cost from how many shapes it holds. An organism that cannot verify itself casts atk 0 and is refused — the pool filter and the mint rule are one line.
card ⊂ organism ⊂ FallKard ⊂ estate — one shape at every scale. The estate grows the breeding stock it is made of.
